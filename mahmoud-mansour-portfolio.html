Cover Letter

Dear Hiring Team,

I am writing to express my interest in roles across Arabic AI data training, data quality analysis, and AI red-teaming and safety. As a native Modern Standard and Egyptian Arabic linguist with over 20 years in language instruction, assessment, and curriculum development, and more than two years in AI evaluation, annotation, and safety review, I bring native fluency, sociocultural insight, and disciplined evaluation methods to each of these areas.

As an Arabic AI data trainer, I work daily at the intersection of language and machine learning. At Cohere I translate multimodal ML and human-authored texts between English and Arabic, label and rank training data in Modern Standard Arabic, and apply International Phonetics to capture dialectal and phonological variation. Across roles at Translated (Uber), Outlier, Invisible Technologies, and Alignerr, I have annotated and evaluated text and audio datasets, recorded dialect-accurate Arabic speech for voice-model training, and contributed to annotation guidelines for dialect and pronunciation tagging. My published Arabic textbooks and research on diglossia and language attitudes ground this work in culturally authentic, dialect-accurate language rather than translated approximations.

As a Senior Data Quality Analyst at Cohere, I evaluate Arabic LLM outputs against detailed style guides and rubrics for accuracy, safety, and consistency, perform response ranking, and flag opportunities to improve data quality and model performance. My work in standards-based assessment reinforces this: I built Arabic reading-comprehension tests for CUNY Queens College, designed standards-aligned curricula for North Carolina Virtual Public Schools, and serve as an ACTFL ILR OPI tester and rater. That experience trained me to calibrate content precisely to difficulty, intent, and explicit criteria, and to document findings with defensible, repeatable reasoning.

On the safety and red-teaming side, my experience is both applied and formally credentialed. I am a certified Red-Teaming Fellow (Mercor), and at Invisible Technologies I conducted safety evaluations on Arabic and English generative outputs, reviewing and categorizing sensitive content such as hate speech, misinformation, and harmful material. At Cohere I label safety content across multiple categories and assess outputs for policy adherence. This pairing of formal red-teaming training with hands-on detection positions me to design and execute adversarial prompts, probe for jailbreaks and policy-evasion failure modes, and translate findings into actionable safety insights with linguistic precision.

What I bring consistently across all three areas is rigor and attention to detail: structured evaluation against clear criteria, accurate documentation, and reliable delivery under deadlines. I would welcome the opportunity, including any screening exam or task, to demonstrate this directly. Thank you for your consideration.
